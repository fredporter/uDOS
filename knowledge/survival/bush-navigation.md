## 1. Southern Hemisphere Sun Navigation

**CRITICAL:** Australia is in Southern Hemisphere - sun moves DIFFERENTLY than Northern Hemisphere

### Sun Position (Southern Hemisphere)

```
East-West Sun Path (Looking NORTH):

         NORTH ▲
               │
     Sunrise   │   Sunset
        ☀──────┼──────☀
      (East)   │   (West)
               │
         YOU (Facing North)

Midday Sun Position:
    Summer (Dec-Feb):  Nearly overhead
    Winter (Jun-Aug):  Lower in sky

    ALWAYS passes through NORTH at noon
    (Opposite of Northern Hemisphere!)

Time vs Direction (AEST):
┌────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 06:00  ☀────→ East (sunrise)          │
│ 09:00    ☀──→ Northeast               │
│ 12:00      ☀ North (directly overhead)│
│ 15:00    ☀──→ Northwest               │
│ 18:00  ☀────→ West (sunset)           │
└────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Shadow Stick Method (Australian Adaptation)

```
Step 1: Place stick vertically (30cm+)
        │
        │ Stick
        │
    ════●════ Ground
        │ Shadow points SOUTH
        ▼ (at noon AEST)

Step 2: Mark shadow tip
        │
        │
    ════●════
        ●─── First mark (09:00)

Step 3: Wait 15 minutes, mark again
        │
        │
    ════●════
        ●───●─── Second mark (09:15)

Step 4: Line between marks = EAST-WEST
        │
        │
    ════●════
        ●═══●  ← EAST-WEST line
         ╲ ╱
          ×
         ╱ ╲
    SOUTH   NORTH

Stand with:
- First mark to your LEFT = Facing NORTH
- First mark to your RIGHT = Facing SOUTH
```

## 2. Natural Navigation (Australian Features)

### Eucalyptus Trees (Unique to Australia)

```
Tree Lean Direction:
      ╔═══╗
      ║ ♠ ║  ← Canopy leans
      ╚═╬═╝    toward sun
        ║╲
        ║ ╲  Prevailing wind
        ║  ╲ (bends away)

East Coast (AEST Grid M360-M410):
- Trees lean WEST (away from ocean wind)
- Stronger on ridges/exposed areas
- Eucalypts show it clearly

Southern Coast (AEST/ACST):
- Prevailing wind: WEST to EAST
- Trees lean EAST
- Coastal she-oaks obvious

Desert (ACST Grid M300-M360):
- Sparse trees, unreliable
- Use other methods
```

## 3. Coastal Navigation

### Beach Features (Australian Coastline)

```
Wave Direction:
    Ocean     Beach
    ≈≈≈≈≈  ───────
    ≈≈≈≈≈  ───────
    ≈≈≈≈≈  ───────

East Coast (AEST):
    Waves from EAST (Pacific)
    Face waves = Facing EAST
    Left = NORTH, Right = SOUTH

South Coast (ACST/AEST):
    Waves from SOUTH (Southern Ocean)
    Face waves = Facing SOUTH
    Left = EAST, Right = WEST

West Coast (AWST):
    Waves from WEST (Indian Ocean)
    Face waves = Facing WEST
    Left = SOUTH, Right = NORTH

Prevailing Swell:
╔════════════════╦════════════════════╗
║ Coast          ║ Wave Direction     ║
╠════════════════╬════════════════════╣
║ NSW/QLD East   ║ East/Southeast     ║
║ VIC South      ║ South/Southwest    ║
║ SA/WA South    ║ South/Southwest    ║
║ WA West        ║ West/Southwest     ║
╚════════════════╩════════════════════╝
```

### Tidal Indicators

```
High/Low Tide Cycle (Approx 6 hours):

High Tide:
    ≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈
    ░░░░░░░░░░░ ← Wet sand line
    ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓ ← Dry sand

Low Tide:
    ≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈
    ▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒ ← Exposed beach
    ░░░░░░░░░░░ ← Wet sand
    ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓ ← Dry sand

Time Navigation:
- Know time of last high tide
- Add 6 hours = next low tide
- Add 12 hours = next high tide
- Plan travel around tides

Major Tidal Locations:
- King Sound WA: 11m range (extreme)
- Sydney Harbour: 1.5m range (moderate)
- Great Australian Bight: 2m range
```

## 5. Emergency: Lost in Bush

### STOP Protocol (Australian Adaptation)

```
S = SIT DOWN
    Don't panic, don't wander
    ═════════
    ●  Sit   Find shade
    ═════════

T = THINK
    Where am I?
    - Last known location
    - Time since lost
    - Direction traveled
    - Resources available

O = OBSERVE
    - Surroundings (landmarks)
    - Sun position
    - Water sources
    - Shelter options
    - Phone signal (check high ground)

P = PLAN
    1. Stay or go?
    2. Water supply?
    3. Shelter needed?
    4. Signal options?
```

### Stay vs Go Decision Matrix

```
STAY PUT if:
╔════════════════════════════════════════╗
║ ✓ Someone knows your location         ║
║ ✓ You have shelter/water              ║
║ ✓ Injured or exhausted                ║
║ ✓ Weather deteriorating               ║
║ ✓ Night approaching                   ║
║ ✓ Lost in dense bush                  ║
║ ✓ Multiple people (stay together)     ║
╚════════════════════════════════════════╝

GO if:
╔════════════════════════════════════════╗
║ ✓ Nobody knows you're missing         ║
║ ✓ No water source at current location ║
║ ✓ You're confident of direction       ║
║ ✓ Heading to known landmark           ║
║ ✓ Following water downstream          ║
║ ✓ Early in day (time to travel)       ║
╚════════════════════════════════════════╝

AUSTRALIAN RULE:
If lost in hot weather = STAY PUT
(Conserve energy, limit water loss)
```

## 6. Following Water (Australian Rivers)

### Downstream Strategy

```
Creek/River Direction:

Mountain:
    ▲▲▲
    ║║║  ← Flows from high to low
    ╚╬╝
     ║
     ║  Creek flows DOWN
     ║
     ▼
    Ocean/Lake

Benefits of following water downstream:
✓ Leads to civilization (usually)
✓ Provides water source
✓ Easier walking (valleys)
✓ More vegetation/food
✓ Wildlife (hunting/trapping)

CAUTION: Australian rivers can:
✗ Dry up (seasonal flow)
✗ Go underground (limestone country)
✗ End in salt lakes (inland drainage)
✗ Flood suddenly (wet season)

Check:
- Is water flowing? (Not dry bed)
- Direction to coast? (Not inland)
- Time of year? (Dry season risk)
```

### Creek Crossing Safety

```
Safe Crossing Method:

Scout for crossing:
    Wide & shallow > Narrow & deep

    GOOD:                 BAD:
    ≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈≈            ╔═══╗
    ░░░░░░░░░ (ankle)    ║≈≈≈║ (deep)
    ═════════            ╚═══╝

Crossing technique:
    1. Remove socks (keep boots on)
    2. Unbuckle pack (ditch if swept)
    3. Use stick upstream for support:

         ●  ← You
        ╱│╲
       ╱ │ ╲
      ●══╬══● Stick
    ≈≈≈≈≈║≈≈≈≈ Water flow →
    ═════●═════

    4. Face upstream
    5. Shuffle sideways (don't cross feet)

NEVER:
✗ Cross in flood
✗ Cross above waterfalls
✗ Cross in fast current (>knee deep)
✗ Cross alone if avoidable
```

## 8. Regional Navigation Strategies

### East Coast Dense Bush (AEST)

**Grid: M360-M410 × 155-210**

```
Strategy:
1. Follow ridges (easier than valleys)
2. Use creeks (lead to civilization)
3. Mark trail (break branches)
4. Climb high for landmarks
5. Listen for roads/trains/ocean

Visibility: ██ Poor (dense vegetation)
Water: ████████ Abundant
Landmarks: ██ Few (trees block view)
```

### Central Desert (ACST)

**Grid: M300-M360 × 165-190**

```
Strategy:
1. Travel dawn/dusk (avoid heat)
2. Use star navigation (clear nights)
3. Follow vehicle tracks (if recent)
4. Head for high ground (scan horizon)
5. Follow birds to water

Visibility: ████████ Excellent
Water: ░░ Scarce (critical factor)
Landmarks: ██████ Good (distant ranges)
```

### Coastal Areas (All Seasons)

**Grid: M280-M440 × Coastal**

```
Strategy:
1. Follow coastline (can't get lost)
2. Stay above high tide mark
3. Use beach for easy walking
4. Signal to boats/aircraft
5. Fresh water in dunes/creeks

Visibility: ██████ Good (ocean reference)
Water: ████ Fair (depends on rain)
Landmarks: ████████ Excellent (coast)
```

## 10. Navigation Safety Rules

### Australian Bush Rules

```
✓ Always tell someone:
  - Where you're going
  - Expected return time
  - Route/grid reference
  - Vehicle details/rego

✓ Carry navigation tools:
  - Phone + backup battery
  - Compass + map
  - Whistle

✓ Know your limits:
  - Distance vs daylight
  - Water requirements
  - Fitness level
  - Weather forecast

✓ Mark your trail:
  - Break branches
  - Stack rocks (cairns)
  - Take photos of landmarks
  - Record waypoints (GPS)

✗ Don't:
  - Travel alone (if possible)
  - Walk at night (unless emergency)
  - Shortcut through unknown terrain
  - Ignore weather warnings
  - Assume phone coverage
```
