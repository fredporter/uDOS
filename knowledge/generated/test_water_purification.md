# uDOS Knowledge Guide: Water Purification

## Overview
Water purification is a critical survival skill <cite>1</cite>. Access to clean water is fundamental for human health and survival across all environments <cite>2</cite>. This guide outlines various methods and considerations for ensuring potable water.

## Methods of Water Purification

### Boiling
Boiling is recognized as one of the most effective methods for purifying water <cite>3</cite>.
*   **Procedure**: Water must be brought to a rolling boil <cite>4</cite>.
*   **Duration**:
    *   At sea level, maintain a rolling boil for at least 1 minute <cite>4</cite>.
    *   At altitudes above 2000 meters, extend the boiling time to 3 minutes <cite>5</cite>.

### Filtration
Physical [[filtration]] removes suspended particles and certain microorganisms from water <cite>6</cite>.
*   **Commercial Filters**: Portable commercial water filters are capable of removing bacteria and protozoa <cite>7</cite>. However, it is important to note that many commercial filters may not effectively remove viruses <cite>8</cite>.
*   **Effectiveness**: Employing multiple [[filtration]] stages significantly improves the overall effectiveness of purification <cite>9</cite>.

### Chemical Treatment
[[Chemical Treatment]] involves the use of disinfectants to kill most pathogens present in water <cite>10</cite>.
*   **Agents**: Common chemical agents include chlorine, iodine, and chlorine dioxide, typically available in tablet or liquid solution form <cite>11</cite>.
*   **Application**: Always adhere strictly to the manufacturer's instructions for proper dosing, which is determined by the volume of water being treated and its contamination level <cite>12</cite>.

## Safety Considerations
Prioritizing safety is paramount when sourcing and purifying water.
*   **Water Quality Assessment**: Always endeavor to test water quality whenever possible <cite>13</cite>.
*   **Source Selection**:
    *   Seek out clear, odorless water, ideally from flowing sources <cite>14</cite>.
    *   Avoid stagnant water <cite>15</cite>.
    *   Do not collect water downstream from known human or animal activity <cite>15</cite>.

## Essential Equipment
Having the correct [[equipment]] greatly enhances water purification capabilities.
*   **Boiling**: A metal container suitable for boiling <cite>16</cite>.
*   **Filtration**: A portable water filter <cite>17</cite>.
*   **Chemical Treatment**: Chemical treatment tablets or liquid solutions <cite>18</cite>.
*   **Storage**: Clean storage containers for purified water <cite>19</cite>.
*   **Pre-filtration**: Filtration cloth or a coffee filter for preliminary particle removal <cite>20</cite>.

## Emergency Situations
In [[Emergency Preparedness]] scenarios where specialized [[equipment]] may be unavailable, alternative strategies are crucial.
*   **Combined Methods**: Utilize a combination of multiple purification methods to maximize effectiveness <cite>21</cite>.
*   **Solar Disinfection (SODIS)**: [[SODIS]] can be an effective method. It involves exposing clear plastic bottles filled with water to direct sunlight for a minimum of 6 hours <cite>22</cite>.
