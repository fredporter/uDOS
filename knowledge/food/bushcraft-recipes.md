## 1. Damper (Bush Bread)

Traditional Australian campfire bread - simple, filling, versatile.

### Ingredients (Serves 4-6)

```
500g self-raising flour (or 500g plain flour + 20g baking powder)
10g salt (2 teaspoons)
50g butter (optional, for richer taste)
300ml water (adjust for consistency)
```

### Method

1. **Mix dry ingredients:**
   - Combine flour and salt in bowl
   - Rub in butter if using (until breadcrumb texture)

2. **Add water gradually:**
   - Make a well in centre
   - Pour in 250ml water
   - Mix with hands, add more water as needed
   - Dough should be soft but not sticky

3. **Shape:**
   - Knead briefly (30 seconds)
   - Form into round flat loaf (5cm thick)
   - Score top with cross pattern

4. **Cook:**

   **Campfire method:**
   - Rake coals to side, leave clear hot spot
   - Place damper on greased metal plate or clean flat rock
   - Cover with pot lid or foil
   - Heap coals on top and around sides
   - Cook 30-40 minutes (sounds hollow when tapped)

   **Bush oven method:**
   - Wrap in foil, bury in coals
   - Cook 25-30 minutes, turn once

   **On a stick:**
   - Divide dough into 6 pieces
   - Roll into long sausages (2cm diameter)
   - Wrap around green stick (spiral)
   - Hold over coals 10-15 minutes, turn frequently

### Variations

- **Cheese damper:** Add 100g grated cheese
- **Herb damper:** Add 2 tablespoons dried herbs
- **Sweet damper:** Add 50g sugar, 50g dried fruit
- **Golden damper:** Replace 100ml water with 100ml milk

## 2. Bush Tea

Simple, comforting, uses local ingredients.

### Eucalyptus Tea

```
5-6 young eucalyptus leaves (fresh)
500ml boiling water
Sugar or honey to taste (optional)
```

**Method:**
1. Wash leaves thoroughly
2. Bruise leaves to release oils
3. Add to billy can with boiling water
4. Steep 5 minutes
5. Strain and serve

**Note:** Use only *young* leaves. Strong antiseptic taste - not for everyone.

### Billy Tea (Black Tea)

```
1L water
10g loose black tea (about 2 tablespoons)
Optional: 50ml milk, sugar to taste
```

**Method:**
1. Boil water in billy can
2. Remove from heat
3. Add tea leaves, stir
4. Let steep 3-4 minutes
5. Add eucalyptus twig and swirl to settle leaves (traditional method)
6. Pour carefully, avoiding leaves
7. Add milk/sugar if desired

## 3. Campfire Porridge

Hot, filling breakfast with endless variations.

### Basic Porridge (Serves 2)

```
100g rolled oats
400ml water (or 200ml water + 200ml milk)
Pinch of salt (2g)
Sweetener to taste
```

**Method:**
1. Bring water to boil in pot
2. Add pinch of salt
3. Stir in oats
4. Reduce to simmer, stir frequently
5. Cook 5-10 minutes until thick and creamy
6. Remove from heat, let stand 1 minute

### Variations

**High-Energy Bush Porridge:**
```
Add to cooked porridge:
- 30g dried fruit (raisins, dates, apricots)
- 20g nuts (chopped almonds, walnuts)
- 15ml honey or 20g brown sugar
- 5g cinnamon
```

**Savoury Porridge:**
```
Add while cooking:
- 5g salt
- 10g butter
- Black pepper to taste
- Optional: 50g grated cheese at end
```

## 4. Simple Stew

One-pot meal, flexible ingredients, warming and nutritious.

### Basic Vegetable Stew (Serves 4)

```
2L water or stock
400g potatoes, diced (2cm cubes)
200g carrots, sliced
200g onions, diced
100g dried beans (soaked overnight) or 1 can (400g)
2 cloves garlic, crushed
10g salt (adjust to taste)
5g black pepper
20g dried herbs (thyme, bay leaf, parsley)
30ml oil or 30g butter
```

**Method:**
1. Heat oil in large pot over fire
2. Fry onions 3-4 minutes until soft
3. Add garlic, fry 1 minute
4. Add water/stock, bring to boil
5. Add potatoes, carrots, beans
6. Add salt, pepper, herbs
7. Simmer 30-40 minutes until vegetables soft
8. Adjust seasoning

### Additions

**With meat (add 300-400g):**
- Brown meat first with onions
- Extend cooking time to 60-90 minutes for tough cuts

**Thickening:**
- Mash some potatoes against side of pot, stir in
- Or mix 30g flour with 100ml cold water, stir in, simmer 5 minutes

## 5. Johnnycakes (Bush Pancakes)

Quick, simple, filling. Cook on any flat surface.

### Ingredients (Makes 8-10 cakes)

```
250g plain flour
10g baking powder
5g salt
20g sugar (optional)
1 egg (or 30ml oil + 20ml extra water)
250ml water or milk
30ml oil for cooking
```

**Method:**
1. Mix flour, baking powder, salt, sugar in bowl
2. Make well in centre
3. Add egg and 200ml water
4. Whisk to smooth batter (add more water if too thick - should pour slowly)
5. Heat oil on flat pan/rock over fire
6. Pour 60ml batter per cake (about ¼ cup)
7. Cook 2-3 minutes until bubbles form and edges dry
8. Flip, cook 1-2 minutes other side
9. Keep warm near fire

### Serving Suggestions

**Sweet:**
- Honey or golden syrup
- Sugar and lemon juice
- Jam
- Fresh berries

**Savoury:**
- As bread with stew
- With fried eggs
- With cheese

## 6. Emergency Survival Soup

Minimal ingredients, maximum nutrition, cooks quickly.

### Basic Recipe (Serves 1-2)

```
1L water
50g rice or pasta
1 stock cube or 10g salt
Whatever vegetables/greens available (handful)
Optional protein: egg, canned fish, dried meat
```

**Method:**
1. Boil water
2. Add stock cube/salt
3. Add rice/pasta, simmer 10-15 minutes
4. Add any vegetables, cook 5 minutes
5. Add protein at end, heat through
6. Eat hot

**Foraged additions** (if confident in identification):
- Dandelion greens
- Nettle tops (wear gloves to pick)
- Wild garlic
- Edible flowers

## Cooking Tips

### Temperature Control

```
Campfire Heat Levels:
─────────────────────────
High:     Hand 10cm above = 2-3 seconds bearable
Medium:   Hand 10cm above = 5-6 seconds bearable
Low:      Hand 10cm above = 8-10 seconds bearable
```

### Altitude Adjustments

```
Above 1000m elevation:
- Water boils at lower temperature
- Increase cooking times by 10-20%
- May need extra water for pasta/rice
```

### Storage

- **Flour:** 6-12 months in sealed container, cool/dry
- **Oats:** 12 months sealed
- **Dried beans:** Years if kept dry
- **Tea:** 12-24 months sealed, dark place
- **Salt:** Indefinite

## Safety

⚠️ **Food Safety Principles:**
- Use clean water (boiled or purified)
- Cook meat thoroughly (no pink, juices clear)
- Don't leave cooked food sitting warm (eat hot or cool quickly)
- Wash hands before cooking
- Keep raw and cooked foods separate

⚠️ **Fire Safety:**
- Clear area around cooking fire
- Keep water nearby
- Never leave fire unattended
- Fully extinguish when done
