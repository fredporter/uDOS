# 72-Hour Bug-Out Bag Checklist

**Purpose:** Prepare a complete emergency evacuation bag for 3 days of self-sufficiency
**Time Required:** 2-3 hours to assemble
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐ Beginner-Intermediate
**Prerequisites:** Access to outdoor/camping supply store, basic budget ($200-500)

---

## Pre-Check: Bag Selection
- [ ] Choose appropriate pack size (40-65L capacity)
- [ ] Verify comfortable shoulder straps and hip belt
- [ ] Check for external attachment points (MOLLE/loops)
- [ ] Ensure water-resistant or has rain cover
- [ ] Test weight when loaded (max 20-25% body weight)

---

## Category 1: Water (Priority 1)

### Water Storage (3L minimum)
- [ ] 2x 1L Nalgene bottles or equivalent (BPA-free)
- [ ] 1x collapsible water container (2L+)
- [ ] 1x metal water bottle or canteen (can boil water)

### Water Purification (Multi-barrier approach)
- [ ] Water filter (Sawyer Mini, LifeStraw, or MSR)
- [ ] 50x water purification tablets (backup)
- [ ] 1x bandana or coffee filter (pre-filter sediment)
- [ ] Small bottle unscented bleach (8 drops/gallon method)

---

## Category 2: Shelter & Warmth (Priority 2)

### Shelter
- [ ] 1x emergency bivvy or space blanket
- [ ] 1x tarp (8x10ft minimum) with grommets
- [ ] 50ft paracord (550lb test)
- [ ] 1x ground pad or closed-cell foam mat
- [ ] Tent (optional, adds weight but comfort)

### Warmth
- [ ] Wool blanket or compact sleeping bag (rated for climate)
- [ ] 2x emergency Mylar blankets (backup)
- [ ] Extra socks (wool, 2-3 pairs)
- [ ] Warm hat/beanie (50% heat loss through head)
- [ ] Gloves (winter) or sun gloves (summer)

### Clothing Layers
- [ ] Base layer (moisture-wicking, not cotton)
- [ ] Mid layer (fleece or wool)
- [ ] Outer layer (rain jacket, windproof)
- [ ] Extra underwear (2-3 sets)
- [ ] Bandana or shemagh (multipurpose)

---

## Category 3: Fire (Priority 3)

### Fire Starting (3+ methods)
- [ ] Lighter (BIC or windproof)
- [ ] Waterproof matches in waterproof container
- [ ] Ferro rod with striker
- [ ] Tinder (dryer lint, cotton balls + petroleum jelly)
- [ ] Small backup candles (light + fire starter)

### Fire Support
- [ ] Small folding saw or wire saw
- [ ] Fatwood or commercial fire starter sticks

---

## Category 4: Food (Priority 4)

### 72-Hour Food Supply
- [ ] 6x energy/protein bars (2 per day)
- [ ] 3x freeze-dried meals (Mountain House, etc.)
- [ ] 1x bag trail mix or nuts (calorie-dense)
- [ ] Instant coffee/tea bags (morale booster)
- [ ] Salt and pepper packets
- [ ] Hard candy (quick energy, morale)

### Cooking
- [ ] Compact camping stove + fuel (optional)
- [ ] Metal cup or pot (can boil water over fire)
- [ ] Spork or utensils
- [ ] Small bottle cooking oil
- [ ] Aluminum foil (cooking, signaling)

---

## Category 5: First Aid & Medical

### First Aid Kit Contents
- [ ] Adhesive bandages (various sizes, 20+)
- [ ] Gauze pads (4x4, sterile, 10+)
- [ ] Medical tape (1 roll)
- [ ] Elastic bandage (ACE wrap)
- [ ] Triangular bandage (sling, tourniquet)
- [ ] Antibiotic ointment packets (10+)
- [ ] Pain relievers (ibuprofen, acetaminophen)
- [ ] Anti-diarrheal medication
- [ ] Antihistamine (allergic reactions)
- [ ] Tweezers and safety pins
- [ ] Medical scissors or trauma shears
- [ ] Nitrile gloves (5+ pairs)
- [ ] CPR face shield
- [ ] Emergency dental kit
- [ ] Prescription medications (7-day supply)

### Personal Medical
- [ ] Glasses/contacts (backup pair)
- [ ] EpiPen (if allergic to bee stings, etc.)
- [ ] Feminine hygiene products
- [ ] Personal medications with instructions

---

## Category 6: Tools & Equipment

### Cutting Tools
- [ ] Fixed-blade knife (4-6" blade, full tang)
- [ ] Multi-tool (Leatherman, Swiss Army)
- [ ] Folding saw (Silky, Bahco)

### Navigation
- [ ] Compass (quality orienteering compass)
- [ ] Local topographic maps (waterproof)
- [ ] GPS device (optional, with backup batteries)
- [ ] Watch (analog preferred for sun navigation)

### Light
- [ ] Headlamp with extra batteries (LED)
- [ ] Backup flashlight (AA or AAA)
- [ ] Glow sticks (chemical backup, 2-3)

### Repair & Maintenance
- [ ] Duct tape (wrap around water bottle)
- [ ] 100ft paracord (additional)
- [ ] Sewing kit (needle, thread, safety pins)
- [ ] Zip ties (10+, various sizes)
- [ ] Super glue (small tube)

---

## Category 7: Communication & Signaling

### Communication
- [ ] Whistle (3 blasts = SOS, louder than shouting)
- [ ] Signal mirror (heliograph, up to 50 miles range)
- [ ] Emergency radio (hand-crank or solar)
- [ ] Cell phone + portable charger (10,000mAh+)
- [ ] Waterproof document pouch

### Signaling
- [ ] Orange surveyor tape (trail marking)
- [ ] Bright-colored tarp or panel (ground-to-air)
- [ ] Flare or smoke signal (optional, check regulations)

---

## Category 8: Documents & Cash

### Important Documents (Waterproof container)
- [ ] Photo ID / Driver's license (copy)
- [ ] Passport (copy)
- [ ] Insurance cards (health, auto, home)
- [ ] Emergency contacts list (written, phone numbers)
- [ ] Medical information (allergies, blood type, conditions)
- [ ] Copies of prescriptions
- [ ] Bank account info (account numbers)
- [ ] Property deed/lease (proof of residence)

### Cash & Credit
- [ ] $200-500 cash in small bills ($1, $5, $20)
- [ ] Credit card (separate from wallet)
- [ ] Coins for pay phones, vending machines

---

## Category 9: Hygiene & Sanitation

### Personal Hygiene
- [ ] Toothbrush + toothpaste (travel size)
- [ ] Biodegradable soap (Dr. Bronner's, camp suds)
- [ ] Quick-dry towel (microfiber)
- [ ] Toilet paper (1 roll, compressed)
- [ ] Hand sanitizer (60%+ alcohol)
- [ ] Wet wipes (baby wipes, multipurpose)
- [ ] Sunscreen (SPF 30+)
- [ ] Lip balm (SPF)
- [ ] Insect repellent (DEET or picaridin)

### Sanitation
- [ ] Trash bags (heavy-duty, 2-3)
- [ ] Ziplock bags (various sizes, 10+)
- [ ] Trowel (bury waste, dig)

---

## Category 10: Miscellaneous Essentials

### General Items
- [ ] Notepad + pencil (waterproof paper ideal)
- [ ] Permanent marker (labeling, messages)
- [ ] 2x large garbage bags (rain gear, shelter, waste)
- [ ] Bungee cords (3-4, various lengths)
- [ ] Carabiners (5+, various sizes)
- [ ] Work gloves (leather or synthetic)
- [ ] Sunglasses (UV protection)
- [ ] Mosquito head net (if applicable)
- [ ] Fishing kit (hooks, line, sinkers - optional)
- [ ] Small Bible, inspirational book, or photos (morale)

---

## Final Assembly & Testing

### Pack Organization
- [ ] Heavy items at bottom and close to back
- [ ] Frequently used items in external pockets
- [ ] Emergency items (first aid, fire) easily accessible
- [ ] Water bottles in side pockets
- [ ] Map and compass in top pocket
- [ ] Test pack weight (≤20-25% body weight)

### Functionality Tests
- [ ] Walk 1 mile with loaded pack (comfort test)
- [ ] Access first aid kit without unpacking everything
- [ ] Deploy shelter/tarp in <10 minutes
- [ ] Start fire with each of 3 methods
- [ ] Filter and purify water sample
- [ ] Locate all essential items in dark (with headlamp)

### Maintenance Schedule
- [ ] Check expiration dates (food, medication, batteries) - Every 6 months
- [ ] Rotate water supply - Every 6 months
- [ ] Replace batteries - Annually
- [ ] Update documents - Annually
- [ ] Practice deployment - Quarterly

---

## Weight Budget Guidelines

**Target Total Weight:** 15-20 lbs (without water)
**With Water:** 20-25 lbs

### Approximate Weights:
- Water (3L): 6.6 lbs
- Food (3 days): 3-4 lbs
- Shelter system: 3-5 lbs
- Clothing: 3-4 lbs
- First aid: 2 lbs
- Tools: 2-3 lbs
- Everything else: 3-4 lbs

**Total:** ~23-30 lbs loaded

---

## Safety Verification

### Before Sealing Bag
- [ ] No items leaking (check fuel, liquids)
- [ ] No expired medications or food
- [ ] Knife and tools secured safely
- [ ] Matches/lighters waterproofed
- [ ] All zippers functional
- [ ] Straps adjusted and tightened
- [ ] Family knows bag location
- [ ] Practice grab-and-go drill (<5 minutes to leave)

### Emergency Scenarios Covered
- [ ] Natural disaster (earthquake, hurricane, flood)
- [ ] Wildfire evacuation
- [ ] Civil unrest or emergency
- [ ] Power grid failure (multi-day)
- [ ] Sudden need to leave home (24+ hours away)

---

## Customization Notes

**Climate Adjustments:**
- **Cold Climate:** Add chemical hand warmers, insulated water bottles, balaclava
- **Hot Climate:** Increase water capacity, add electrolyte powder, sun hat
- **Wet Climate:** Upgrade rain gear, waterproof everything, quick-dry clothing

**Personal Needs:**
- **Children:** Smaller pack, comfort items, extra snacks, activities
- **Elderly:** Medications, mobility aids, larger first aid kit
- **Pets:** Pet food, bowls, leash, vaccination records

**Urban vs. Wilderness:**
- **Urban:** Cash, maps, dust mask (N95), crowbar/pry bar
- **Wilderness:** Enhanced navigation, signal devices, fishing kit
