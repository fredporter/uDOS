# First Aid Kit Inventory Checklist

**Purpose:** Assemble a comprehensive wilderness/emergency first aid kit
**Time Required:** 1-2 hours to gather and organize
**Difficulty:** ⭐ Beginner
**Prerequisites:** Access to pharmacy and outdoor supply store, budget ($50-150)

---

## Kit Size Selection

Choose based on intended use:
- [ ] **Personal (Small)** - Individual day trips, pocket kit, 20-30 items
- [ ] **Group (Medium)** - 2-5 people, 1-3 days, 50-75 items
- [ ] **Expedition (Large)** - 6+ people, multi-day, 100+ items, includes advanced supplies

*This checklist covers MEDIUM group kit. Adjust quantities as needed.*

---

## Container Requirements

- [ ] Waterproof container or dry bag
- [ ] Clear compartments or ziplock organization
- [ ] Red cross symbol or "FIRST AID" label
- [ ] Lightweight (≤2-3 lbs when full)
- [ ] Easy one-handed access
- [ ] Inventory list inside lid

---

## Category 1: Wound Care

### Bandages & Dressings
- [ ] Adhesive bandages assorted (30+)
  - Small: 20x (fingertip, knuckle)
  - Medium: 10x (standard strips)
  - Large: 5x (2x3 inch patches)
- [ ] Butterfly closures (10+) - Close small lacerations
- [ ] Gauze pads sterile (15+)
  - 2x2 inch: 5x
  - 4x4 inch: 10x
- [ ] Non-adherent pads (5x) - Burns, abrasions
- [ ] Rolled gauze (3 rolls, various widths)
- [ ] Trauma pads (2x, 5x9 inch) - Large wounds

### Securing & Support
- [ ] Medical tape (2 rolls)
  - 1x cloth tape (1 inch wide)
  - 1x paper tape (1/2 inch, sensitive skin)
- [ ] Elastic bandage/ACE wrap (2 rolls)
  - 1x 2-inch width
  - 1x 4-inch width
- [ ] Triangular bandage (2x) - Sling, tourniquet, head wrap
- [ ] Self-adhering wrap (Coban, 2 rolls) - Sprains, secure dressings
- [ ] Safety pins (12+, various sizes)

---

## Category 2: Wound Treatment

### Cleaning & Disinfection
- [ ] Antiseptic wipes (20+, alcohol or benzalkonium)
- [ ] Providone-iodine solution (small bottle, 10%)
- [ ] Hydrogen peroxide (small bottle, 3%) - Debris removal
- [ ] Saline wound wash (spray bottle or individual packets)
- [ ] Antibacterial soap (small bottle, unscented)

### Topical Medications
- [ ] Triple antibiotic ointment (15+ packets or 1 tube)
- [ ] Hydrocortisone cream 1% (tube) - Itching, rash, insect bites
- [ ] Burn gel or aloe vera (tube) - Burns, sunburn
- [ ] Moleskin or blister pads (5+ sheets) - Hotspots, blisters

---

## Category 3: Instruments & Tools

### Cutting & Removal
- [ ] Medical scissors or trauma shears (1 pair, rust-resistant)
- [ ] Tweezers (1 pair, fine point) - Splinters, ticks
- [ ] Tick removal tool (optional but recommended)
- [ ] Scalpel blade (#11, sterile, 2x) - Advanced use only
- [ ] Razor blade (single-edge, 2x) - Shaving, tape cutting

### Diagnostic Tools
- [ ] Digital thermometer (1x, waterproof if possible)
- [ ] Penlight or small LED flashlight
- [ ] Blood pressure cuff (optional, expedition kits)
- [ ] Stethoscope (optional, if trained)

---

## Category 4: Medications (OTC)

### Pain Relief
- [ ] Ibuprofen 200mg (20+ tablets) - Pain, fever, inflammation
- [ ] Acetaminophen 500mg (20+ tablets) - Pain, fever (liver-safe)
- [ ] Aspirin 325mg (10+ tablets) - Pain, heart attack response

### Gastrointestinal
- [ ] Anti-diarrheal (Imodium, 10+ tablets)
- [ ] Laxative (5+ doses, for opioid-induced constipation)
- [ ] Antacid tablets (Tums, 10+) - Heartburn, calcium source
- [ ] Anti-nausea (Dramamine, 10+ tablets) - Motion sickness

### Allergy & Respiratory
- [ ] Antihistamine (Benadryl 25mg, 20+ tablets) - Allergies, sleep aid
- [ ] Decongestant (Sudafed, 10+ tablets) - Sinus, altitude
- [ ] Cough drops or lozenges (1 pack)
- [ ] EpiPen (2x, if anyone has severe allergies) - CRITICAL

### Topical & Other
- [ ] Eye drops (saline, sterile, 2x vials)
- [ ] Oral rehydration salts (5+ packets) - Dehydration, diarrhea
- [ ] Electrolyte powder (emergency, 5+ packets)
- [ ] Glucose tablets or gel (diabetic emergency, 10+ tablets)

---

## Category 5: Specialized Trauma Supplies

### Bleeding Control
- [ ] Tourniquet (CAT or SOFTT, 1-2x) - Life-threatening limb bleeding
- [ ] Hemostatic gauze (QuikClot, 2x) - Severe bleeding
- [ ] Israeli bandage or pressure dressing (2x)
- [ ] Nitrile gloves (10+ pairs, various sizes) - Infection control

### Fracture & Immobilization
- [ ] SAM splint (1x, moldable aluminum) - Fractures, sprains
- [ ] Finger splints (2x, various sizes)
- [ ] Sling (triangular bandage serves double duty)

### Airway & Breathing
- [ ] CPR face shield (2x) - Barrier protection
- [ ] Nasopharyngeal airway (1x, if trained) - Unconscious patient
- [ ] Chest seal (2x, vented) - Penetrating chest wounds

---

## Category 6: Environmental & Special Needs

### Heat & Cold Injuries
- [ ] Instant cold packs (3x) - Sprains, heat exhaustion
- [ ] Instant heat packs (3x) - Hypothermia, frostbite (DO NOT apply directly to skin)
- [ ] Space blanket/emergency bivvy (2x) - Hypothermia, shock
- [ ] Electrolyte powder (already listed above)

### Burns & Sun
- [ ] Burn dressings (2x, sterile, non-adherent)
- [ ] Sunscreen SPF 30+ (small bottle)
- [ ] Lip balm with SPF (2x sticks)
- [ ] After-sun aloe gel (already listed above)

### Bites & Stings
- [ ] Sting relief pads (10+, benzocaine or hydrocortisone)
- [ ] Sawyer Extractor (snake/spider bite, controversial efficacy)
- [ ] Tick removal tool (already listed above)
- [ ] Benadryl (already listed above) - Allergic reactions

### Dental Emergency
- [ ] Temporary filling material (Dentemp)
- [ ] Dental floss (multi-purpose)
- [ ] Oil of cloves (toothache pain relief)
- [ ] Gauze for packing (already listed above)

---

## Category 7: Hygiene & Sanitation

### Infection Prevention
- [ ] Hand sanitizer (60%+ alcohol, 2oz bottle)
- [ ] Antibacterial wipes (20+)
- [ ] Nitrile gloves (already listed, but ensure 10+ pairs)
- [ ] Biohazard bag (2x, red, for contaminated waste)

### Personal Protection
- [ ] Face masks (N95 or surgical, 5+) - Airborne illness, dust
- [ ] Eye protection (safety glasses, 1 pair) - Blood splash
- [ ] Disposable gowns or large plastic bags (2x)

---

## Category 8: Documentation & Reference

### Medical Information
- [ ] Personal medical info cards (allergies, blood type, conditions)
- [ ] Emergency contacts list (written, laminated)
- [ ] Medication list (prescriptions, doses)
- [ ] Consent forms (for treating minors)

### Reference Materials
- [ ] Wilderness First Aid pocket guide (laminated cards)
- [ ] CPR instructions card (current AHA/Red Cross)
- [ ] Medication dosage chart (by age/weight)
- [ ] Inventory checklist (this document, laminated)

---

## Category 9: Prescription Medications

### Personal Medications (7-14 day supply)
- [ ] Blood pressure medication
- [ ] Diabetes medication (insulin + syringes)
- [ ] Asthma inhaler
- [ ] Anti-anxiety or psychiatric medications
- [ ] Antibiotics (if prescribed for trip)
- [ ] Pain medication (prescription strength)
- [ ] Any chronic condition medications

### Prescription-Strength Additions (if authorized)
- [ ] Epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen, 2x)
- [ ] Albuterol inhaler (asthma, altitude)
- [ ] Oral antibiotics (broad-spectrum, if doctor approves)
- [ ] Prescription pain medication (Tramadol, etc.)

---

## Kit Organization

### Compartment Layout
- [ ] **Red compartment** - Life-threatening: Tourniquet, hemostatic gauze, chest seal
- [ ] **Yellow compartment** - Wound care: Gauze, bandages, tape
- [ ] **Green compartment** - Medications: Pills organized by type
- [ ] **Blue compartment** - Tools: Scissors, tweezers, thermometer
- [ ] **Clear bags** - Individual use items (gloves, antiseptic wipes)

### Accessibility Priority
- [ ] Most critical items on top or external pocket
- [ ] Gloves accessible without opening main kit
- [ ] Tourniquet on exterior (seconds matter)
- [ ] Inventory list visible inside lid
- [ ] Expiration dates facing outward

---

## Weight & Size Guidelines

### Target Weights by Kit Size:
- **Personal:** 0.5-1 lb
- **Group:** 2-3 lbs
- **Expedition:** 4-6 lbs

### Container Dimensions:
- **Personal:** 6x4x2 inches
- **Group:** 10x8x4 inches
- **Expedition:** 12x10x6 inches or larger

---

## Verification & Testing

### Inventory Check
- [ ] Count all items against checklist
- [ ] Verify quantities sufficient for group size
- [ ] Check expiration dates (medications, ointments, sterile items)
- [ ] Ensure nothing leaking or damaged
- [ ] Test zippers and closures

### Accessibility Drill
- [ ] Find and deploy tourniquet in <10 seconds
- [ ] Access gloves without unpacking kit
- [ ] Locate trauma pads in <20 seconds
- [ ] Read medication labels in low light
- [ ] Open all containers one-handed (simulate injury)

### Training Requirements
- [ ] All group members know kit location
- [ ] At least one person has WFA or WFR certification
- [ ] Group familiar with tourniquet use
- [ ] CPR training current (within 2 years)
- [ ] Practice scenarios conducted (quarterly)

---

## Maintenance Schedule

### Every Trip
- [ ] Verify kit is packed and accessible
- [ ] Check for recently expired items
- [ ] Resupply any items used on previous trip
- [ ] Ensure batteries in thermometer/penlight functional

### Monthly
- [ ] Inspect for damage, leaks, or mold
- [ ] Rotate medications approaching expiration
- [ ] Check glove integrity (holes, deterioration)
- [ ] Verify sterile packaging intact

### Every 6 Months
- [ ] Full inventory against checklist
- [ ] Replace all expired medications
- [ ] Replace all expired sterile items
- [ ] Update personal medical information
- [ ] Refresh training on critical items

### Annually
- [ ] Complete kit review and reorganization
- [ ] Replace medications within 1 year of expiration
- [ ] Update reference materials (new guidelines)
- [ ] Renew first aid certification
- [ ] Practice full trauma scenario

## Customization by Environment

### Desert/Hot Climate Additions
- [ ] Extra electrolyte powder (increase 3x)
- [ ] Sunscreen higher SPF (50+)
- [ ] Cooling towels or neck wraps
- [ ] Snake bite kit (controversial, know local species)
- [ ] Extra water purification capability

### Cold/Winter Additions
- [ ] Frostbite treatment guide
- [ ] More heat packs (increase 5x)
- [ ] Petroleum jelly (chapped skin, cold injuries)
- [ ] Lip balm (increase quantity)
- [ ] Chemical hand warmers

### Tropical/Jungle Additions
- [ ] Anti-fungal powder or cream
- [ ] Extra antibiotic ointment (infection risk higher)
- [ ] Malaria prophylaxis (if prescribed)
- [ ] Insect repellent (DEET 30%+)
- [ ] Anti-diarrheal (increase quantity)

### High Altitude Additions
- [ ] Acetazolamide (Diamox, for altitude sickness)
- [ ] Extra pain relievers (headache common)
- [ ] Sunscreen higher SPF (UV stronger)
- [ ] Lip balm with high SPF
- [ ] Nosebleed supplies (dry air)

---

## Legal & Ethical Considerations

### Scope of Practice
- [ ] Only use items/techniques you're trained for
- [ ] Prescription medications: Personal use only (legal exception: EpiPen for anaphylaxis)
- [ ] Obtain consent before treating others (verbal OK in emergency)
- [ ] Document all treatments provided
- [ ] Know Good Samaritan laws in your region

### Controlled Substances
- [ ] Prescription medications in original labeled containers
- [ ] Carry doctor's note for Schedule II-IV medications (narcotics, stimulants)
- [ ] Syringes: Include prescription label for insulin/EpiPen
- [ ] International travel: Check destination country regulations
