## Survival Notes

### Earthquake Preparedness

Tokyo is in a major seismic zone. Earthquakes are common.

**During an Earthquake**:

1. **DROP** - Get low
2. **COVER** - Under table or desk
3. **HOLD ON** - Until shaking stops
4. Do NOT run outside during shaking
5. After: Check for injuries, avoid elevators

**Emergency Kit (Recommended)**:

- [ ] Water (3L per person per day)
- [ ] Flashlight + batteries
- [ ] First aid kit
- [ ] Cash (ATMs may be down)
- [ ] Phone charger
- [ ] Whistle (for rescue)

## Transport

### Rail Network

Tokyo has one of the world's most extensive rail networks:

| Type | Operator | Coverage |
| ---- | -------- | -------- |
| Metro | Tokyo Metro | 13 lines, city centre |
| Subway | Toei | 4 lines, supplements Metro |
| JR | Japan Railways | Yamanote loop + suburbs |
| Private | Various | Suburban connections |

### Key Stations

- **Shinjuku** - World's busiest (3.5M/day)
- **Shibuya** - Major interchange
- **Tokyo** - Shinkansen hub
- **Ikebukuro** - Northwest hub
- **Ueno** - Northeast, museums

### IC Cards

**Suica** or **Pasmo** - Rechargeable smart cards for all trains, buses, and many shops. Essential for travel.

### Tips

- Rush hour: 7:30-9:30am, 5:30-8:00pm
- Women-only cars available (marked pink)
- No eating/drinking on trains
- Quiet coaches on Shinkansen

## Local Knowledge

### Cash Society

Many places still prefer cash. ATMs in 7-Eleven accept foreign cards.

### Convenience Stores (Konbini)

7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart - Open 24/7, sell:

- Hot food, onigiri, bento
- ATM services
- Tickets, payments
- Basic supplies
- Free WiFi

### Addresses

Japanese addresses are complex. Use Google Maps or have address written in Japanese.

### Tipping

**Do not tip** - Considered rude in Japan.
