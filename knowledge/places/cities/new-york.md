## Survival Notes

### Climate Adaptation

NYC has extreme seasonal variation:

- **Summer (Jun-Aug)**: Hot, humid, 25-35¬∞C, heat waves
- **Autumn (Sep-Nov)**: Pleasant, 10-20¬∞C, beautiful
- **Winter (Dec-Feb)**: Cold, -5 to 5¬∞C, snow, wind chill
- **Spring (Mar-May)**: Variable, 5-20¬∞C

### Extreme Weather Preparation

**Summer Heat**:

- [ ] Stay hydrated
- [ ] Seek air conditioning
- [ ] Cooling centers open during heat waves
- [ ] Avoid midday exertion

**Winter Cold**:

- [ ] Layer clothing
- [ ] Cover extremities (hat, gloves)
- [ ] Wind chill can be severe
- [ ] Watch for ice

### Hazards

| Hazard | Season | Mitigation |
| ------ | ------ | ---------- |
| Heat wave | Summer | AC, hydration, rest |
| Blizzard | Winter | Stay indoors, supplies |
| Hurricane | Aug-Oct | Monitor forecasts |
| Flash flooding | Year-round | Avoid low areas |
| Wind chill | Winter | Cover exposed skin |

### Water Sources

- **Tap water**: Among best in US
- **Hydrants**: Emergency only
- **Bodegas**: Everywhere, 24/7
- **Water fountains**: Parks, some stations

### Shelter

- **Subway**: 24/7, warm in winter
- **Public libraries**: Free, Wi-Fi
- **Malls**: Penn Station shops
- **Museums**: Many have suggested donation

## The Grid System

Manhattan is laid out on a grid:

- **Streets** run east-west (numbered, increasing northward)
- **Avenues** run north-south (numbered west to east)
- **5th Avenue** divides East/West
- Below 14th St: chaotic, use GPS

**Walking estimates**:

- 20 blocks = ~1 mile (north-south)
- 1 crosstown block = ~3 regular blocks

## Local Knowledge

### Bodegas

Small corner stores, open 24/7:

- Sandwiches, coffee
- Basic supplies
- ATMs
- Phone chargers

**Bacon, Egg & Cheese** (BEC) is the NYC breakfast classic.

### Pizza

By the slice is the NYC way. Fold it.

### Bagels

Real NYC bagels are boiled then baked. Order "with a schmear" (cream cheese).

### Tipping

| Service | Tip |
| ------- | --- |
| Restaurants | 18-20% |
| Bars | $1-2 per drink |
| Taxis | 15-20% |
| Delivery | 15-20% |
| Coffee counter | Not expected |

### Street Smarts

- Walk with purpose
- Stay right on sidewalks
- Don't block subway doors
- Avoid eye contact with aggressive people
- Keep valuables secure
