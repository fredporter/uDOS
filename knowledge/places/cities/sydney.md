## Survival Notes

### Climate Adaptation

Sydney has a humid subtropical climate with distinct seasons:

- **Summer (Dec-Feb)**: Hot, 25-35¬∞C, high UV, bushfire risk
- **Autumn (Mar-May)**: Mild, 18-25¬∞C, pleasant
- **Winter (Jun-Aug)**: Cool, 8-17¬∞C, occasional rain
- **Spring (Sep-Nov)**: Warm, 15-25¬∞C, variable

### Essential Gear

- [ ] Sunscreen SPF 30+
- [ ] Hat (broad-brimmed)
- [ ] Sunglasses
- [ ] Water bottle
- [ ] Light rain jacket (year-round)

### Hazards

| Hazard | Season | Mitigation |
| ------ | ------ | ---------- |
| Bushfire | Summer | Check fire.nsw.gov.au, avoid bush |
| Heat | Summer | Hydrate, seek shade, limit exertion |
| UV Index | Year-round | Cover up 10am-2pm |
| Rip currents | Year-round | Swim between flags only |
| Spiders/Snakes | Year-round | Shake shoes, watch where stepping |

### Water Sources

- **Tap water**: Safe throughout Sydney
- **Public fountains**: Parks, beaches, CBD
- **Bubblers**: Common in public spaces

### Emergency Shelters

- Public libraries (air-conditioned)
- Shopping centres
- Train stations
- Hospital emergency departments

## Local Knowledge

### Slip, Slop, Slap

Australian sun safety campaign:
- **Slip** on a shirt
- **Slop** on sunscreen
- **Slap** on a hat
- **Seek** shade
- **Slide** on sunnies

### Swim Between the Flags

Red and yellow flags mark the safest swimming area patrolled by lifeguards. **Never swim outside the flags**.

### Fire Danger Ratings

Check daily during summer:
- **Catastrophic** - Stay home, protect property
- **Extreme** - Avoid bushland completely
- **Very High** - Be alert, avoid remote areas
