## 3. Rainwater Collection (Wet Season)

### Northern Australia Wet Season (Oct-Apr)

**Grid: M390-M440 × 140-165 (AEST)**

```
Tarp Collection System:
    ┌────────────────┐
    │  Tarp 2×2m     │ ← Angle 20-30°
    └───────┬────────┘
            │ Gutter/channel
            ▼
    ┌──────────────┐
    │ ▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒▒ │ ← Container
    │ ░Clean░water░ │
    └──────────────┘

Wet season rainfall (Nov-Mar):
Daily storms = 50-200mm per day

Collection capacity:
1mm rain × 1m² tarp = 1 liter
Heavy storm (100mm) × 2m² = 200 liters

First rain flush:
    Rain 1:  ░░░░░░ Dirty (dust/debris)
    Wait 5 min, discard first liter

    Rain 2:  ▒▒▒▒▒▒ Cleaner
    Collect for drinking (still treat)
```

### Salinity (Inland Water)

```
Salt Test:
    Evaporation test (if time):

    Water sample ░░░░░ in container
    Leave in sun 2-4 hours
           │
           ▼
    ░░░░░ Evaporates
    ═════ Check residue

    WHITE CRUST ▓▓▓ = High salt (unsafe)

    Salty taste = Too much salt

• Inland drainage basins
• Old lake beds
• Bore water (some areas)
```

## 6. Emergency Extraction Methods

### Solar Still (Outback Survival)

```
Construction:
    Sun ☀️
     │││
     ▼▼▼
  ╔═══════════╗
  ║  Plastic  ║ ← Clear plastic sheet
  ╚═════╦═════╝   secured with rocks
        ║
  ▓▓▓▓▓●▓▓▓▓▓ ← Weight (small rock)
  ▒▒▒▒▒║▒▒▒▒▒   on plastic
  ░░░░░╚░░░░░ ← Hole 1m deep
  ═══════●═══ ← Container (center)
  │ ≈≈≈≈≈≈≈ │   collects water
  └─────────┘

Process:
1. Dig hole 1m wide × 1m deep
2. Place container in center
3. Optional: Add vegetation/urine around
4. Cover with plastic sheet
5. Seal edges with dirt
6. Weight center over container
7. Wait 24 hours

Yield: 0.5-1.5 liters per day
Best: Hot, sunny conditions
Grid: M300-M350 × 170-185 (Desert)
```

### Transpiration Bag (Australian Native Plants)

```
Setup:
    Clear plastic bag
    ╔═══════════════╗
    ║ ░░░░░░░░░░░░░ ║
    ╚═══════╦═══════╝
      ♠♠♠♠♠║♠♠♠♠♠ ← Eucalyptus branch
      ║║║║║║║║║║║║║   (living, leafy)
      ╚═════●═══════
            │
    Water collects in corner

Best trees:
• River Red Gum (high water content)
• Coolabah
• Any eucalyptus with green leaves
• NOT desert species (low yield)

Process:
1. Select leafy branch (full sun)
2. Enclose in clear plastic bag
3. Seal bag around branch (tape/string)
4. Weight one corner down
5. Wait 2-4 hours

Yield: 50-200ml per bag per day
Use multiple bags for more water
```
