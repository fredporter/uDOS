## Content Guidelines

### ✅ Include
- **Evidence-based** practices backed by research
- **Accessible** techniques anyone can try
- **Clear** instructions for implementation
- **When to seek professional help**
- **Multiple approaches** (what works varies by person)

### ❌ Exclude
- Medical advice (always recommend consulting professionals)
- Cure-all promises or miracle solutions
- Unproven alternative medicine claims
- Toxic positivity ("just think positive!")
- Diet culture and body shaming
- Pseudo-science and unfounded claims

## Well-Being Philosophy

**Human-centric well-being means**:
- Mental and physical health are equally important
- No one-size-fits-all solutions
- Professional help is strength, not weakness
- Prevention is easier than cure
- Small, sustainable changes over dramatic transformations
- Well-being enables everything else

## Important Disclaimers

**This knowledge is for educational purposes only.**

- ⚠️ **Not medical advice** - Always consult healthcare professionals
- ⚠️ **Emergency situations** - Call emergency services immediately
- ⚠️ **Mental health crisis** - Contact crisis hotlines (988 in US)
- ⚠️ **Medication** - Never start/stop without doctor guidance

## Contributing

When adding well-being knowledge:
1. **Cite sources** - Link to peer-reviewed research when possible
2. **Include disclaimers** - Make clear what requires professional help
3. **Test personally** (where safe) before documenting
4. **Avoid jargon** - Explain in plain language
5. **Update** as new research emerges

## Crisis Resources

**If you or someone else is in crisis:**

- **US**: 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
- **International**: https://findahelpline.com
- **Emergency**: Call local emergency services (911, 112, 999, etc.)
