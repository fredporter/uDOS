# Basic CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

**Category**: Survival > First Aid
**Last Updated**: November 14, 2025
**Source**: International resuscitation guidelines

> ⚠️ **Emergency**: If someone is unresponsive and not breathing, call emergency services (911/112/999) IMMEDIATELY, then begin CPR.

---

## When to Use CPR

CPR is needed when someone is:
- **Unresponsive** (doesn't respond to shaking or shouting)
- **Not breathing** or only gasping
- **No pulse** (if you can check)

**If unsure whether to start CPR, start it.** It's better to do CPR on someone who doesn't need it than to not do it on someone who does.

---

## Before You Start

1. **Check scene safety** - Don't put yourself in danger
2. **Check responsiveness** - Tap shoulders, shout "Are you okay?"
3. **Call for help** - Dial emergency services OR send someone to call
4. **Get AED if available** - Automated External Defibrillator

---

## CPR Steps (Hands-Only for Adults)

### 1. Position the Person
- Lay them flat on their back on a firm surface
- Kneel beside their chest

### 2. Hand Placement
- Place heel of one hand in center of chest (between nipples)
- Place other hand on top
- Interlock fingers
- Keep arms straight, shoulders directly over hands

### 3. Compressions
- **Push hard and fast**
- Depth: At least 2 inches (5 cm) for adults
- Rate: 100-120 compressions per minute
- **Rhythm**: Think of song "Stayin' Alive" by Bee Gees (103 BPM)

### 4. Allow Chest to Recoil
- Let chest return to normal position between compressions
- Don't lean on the chest

### 5. Continue Until
- Emergency services arrive
- Person starts breathing normally
- AED is ready to use
- You're too exhausted to continue

---

## With Rescue Breaths (Trained Rescuers)

If you're trained in CPR with rescue breaths:

**30:2 Ratio**
- 30 chest compressions
- 2 rescue breaths
- Repeat cycle

### Giving Rescue Breaths
1. Tilt head back, lift chin
2. Pinch nose closed
3. Make complete seal over mouth
4. Give 1 breath (1 second), watch chest rise
5. Give second breath
6. If chest doesn't rise, reposition airway and try again
7. Don't interrupt compressions for more than 10 seconds

---

## CPR for Children (1-8 years)

**Same as adults, but:**
- Use one or two hands for compressions
- Compress at least 1/3 depth of chest (about 2 inches / 5 cm)
- Same rate: 100-120 compressions/minute

---

## CPR for Infants (Under 1 year)

**Different technique:**
- Use **two fingers** in center of chest (just below nipple line)
- Compress at least 1/3 depth of chest (about 1.5 inches / 4 cm)
- Rate: 100-120 compressions/minute
- For breaths: cover both nose AND mouth with your mouth

---

## Using an AED (Automated External Defibrillator)

If AED is available:

1. **Turn on AED** - Follow voice prompts
2. **Bare the chest** - Remove clothing, dry if wet
3. **Attach pads** - Place as shown on pads
4. **Stand clear** - Don't touch person during analysis
5. **Deliver shock if advised** - Press button when told
6. **Resume CPR immediately** - Start with compressions
7. **Continue** until help arrives or person recovers

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Compressions too shallow** - Push at least 2 inches deep
❌ **Compressions too slow** - 100-120 per minute (fast!)
❌ **Leaning on chest** - Allow full recoil
❌ **Too many interruptions** - Minimize pauses in compressions
❌ **Wrong hand position** - Center of chest, not too low
❌ **Giving up too soon** - Continue until help arrives

---

## After CPR

Even if person recovers:
- **They still need medical evaluation**
- Stay with them until help arrives
- Monitor breathing and responsiveness
- Be prepared to resume CPR if needed

---

## Getting Trained

**This guide is NOT a substitute for proper training.**

**Get certified in CPR through:**
- Australian Resuscitation Council
- International resuscitation guidelines
- St John Ambulance Australia
- Local hospitals or fire departments
- Online + in-person hybrid courses

**Certification typically includes:**
- Adult, child, and infant CPR
- AED use
- Choking response
- Hands-on practice with mannequins

**Recertification**: Every 2 years (skills deteriorate quickly)
