# Environment Knowledge

**Category Focus**: Sustainable living, ecological awareness, and harmonious relationship with nature.

## What Belongs Here

- **Gardening** - Growing food, flowers, and herbs
- **Conservation** - Protecting and restoring ecosystems
- **Renewable energy** - Solar, wind, and alternative power

## Content Guidelines

### ✅ Include
- **Practical** techniques you can implement
- **Local adaptation** considerations
- **Resource efficiency** (minimal waste, maximum benefit)
- **DIY solutions** with accessible materials
- **Long-term thinking** (sustainability)
- **Science-based** approaches

### ❌ Exclude
- Greenwashing or corporate sustainability theater
- Unproven or pseudoscientific claims
- Expensive solutions only accessible to wealthy
- Political ideology (focus on practical action)
- Judgmental or preachy tone
- One-size-fits-all mandates

## Environmental Philosophy

**Human-centric environmentalism means**:
- People and planet both matter
- Small actions compound over time
- Perfect is enemy of good (do what you can)
- Local action has global impact
- Traditional knowledge is valuable
- Technology and nature can coexist
- Resilience over mere efficiency

## Getting Started

### Complete Beginner
1. **Start composting** - Turn waste into soil
2. **Grow herbs** - Easy windowsill gardening
3. **Reduce energy** - Simple efficiency improvements
4. **Learn local ecology** - What grows naturally near you?

### Some Experience
1. **Vegetable garden** - Grow some of your own food
2. **Rainwater catchment** - Harvest free water
3. **Solar experiment** - Small off-grid project
4. **Wildlife habitat** - Support pollinators and birds

### Advanced Practitioner
1. **Food forest** - Perennial food production
2. **Renewable energy system** - Significant solar/wind setup
3. **Closed-loop systems** - Waste = food for other systems
4. **Teaching others** - Spread knowledge and skills

## Contributing

When adding environmental knowledge:
1. **Include climate zones** - What works where?
2. **Show realistic timelines** - Gardens take time
3. **Cost breakdowns** - What's the investment?
4. **Failure modes** - What can go wrong?
5. **Local resources** - Where to get materials, plants, help
