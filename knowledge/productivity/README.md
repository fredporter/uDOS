# Productivity Knowledge

**Category Focus**: Systems, workflows, and tools for getting things done efficiently and effectively.

## What Belongs Here

Practical knowledge for:
- **Time management** - Scheduling, prioritization, focus techniques
- **Workflows** - Processes for common tasks
- **Tools** - Software, hardware, and systems for productivity
- **Automation** - Reducing repetitive work
- **Personal knowledge management** - Note-taking, organizing information

## Subcategories

### time-management/
Making the most of your time:
- Pomodoro and focus techniques
- Calendar and scheduling systems
- Task prioritization frameworks (Eisenhower Matrix, etc.)
- Deep work vs shallow work
- Energy management
- Procrastination solutions
- Time tracking and analysis

### workflows/
Proven processes for common tasks:
- Writing and editing workflow
- Research and learning process
- Project planning and execution
- Meeting and communication protocols
- Email and message management
- File organization systems
- Backup and archiving procedures

### tools/
Productivity software and hardware:
- Text editors and note-taking apps
- Task managers and to-do systems
- Calendar and scheduling apps
- Automation tools (scripts, shortcuts)
- Hardware recommendations (keyboards, displays, etc.)
- Command-line productivity tools
- Comparison guides and reviews

### automation/
Eliminating repetitive work:
- Shell scripts and automation
- Text expansion and snippets
- Keyboard shortcuts and macros
- Scheduled tasks and cron jobs
- Workflow automation (IFTTT-style)
- Batch processing techniques
- Template systems

## Content Guidelines

### ✅ Include
- **Actionable** systems you can implement today
- **Principles** that work across tools and contexts
- **Comparisons** of different approaches
- **Real examples** from actual workflows
- **Time estimates** for implementing systems

### ❌ Exclude
- Generic motivation content ("10 habits of successful people")
- Productivity porn (complex systems that don't actually help)
- Tool-specific tutorials (unless comparing approaches)
- Hustle culture / overwork promotion
- Unproven "hacks" without evidence

## Productivity Philosophy

**Human-centric productivity means**:
- Work smarter, not necessarily harder
- Systems that reduce stress, not add complexity
- Focus on outcomes, not just output
- Sustainable practices, not burnout
- Clarity over busyness

## Contributing

When adding productivity knowledge:
1. **Test** systems yourself before documenting
2. **Include** both benefits AND limitations
3. **Adapt** for different contexts (remote, office, creative, analytical)
4. **Update** when tools or practices evolve
5. **Focus** on principles that transfer across tools
