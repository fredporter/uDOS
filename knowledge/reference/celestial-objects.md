Celestial objects populate our night sky and cosmos. Understanding their types, scales, and characteristics enhances navigation ability, scientific literacy, and philosophical perspective. This guide covers the major categories visible from Earth.

## Observable Objects by Naked Eye

### Minimum Equipment Visibility

| Object Type | Naked Eye | Binoculars | Small Telescope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stars** | Down to +6.0 mag | Down to +10 mag | Down to +13 mag |
| **Planets** | All visible | Enhanced view | Surface detail |
| **Moon** | Clear view | Craters visible | Formations clear |
| **Star Clusters** | M45 (Pleiades) | M13 (Great Cluster) | Resolved stars |
| **Nebulae** | Orion, Crab faint | Orion detail visible | Structure visible |
| **Galaxies** | M31, M33 faint | M31 halo visible | Detail visible |
| **Comets** | When bright | Extended tails | Fine structure |

## Night Sky Safety

### Protecting Your Night Vision

1. **Avoid white light** â€” Use red headlamp or filtered flashlight
2. **Let eyes adjust** â€” 20-30 minutes minimum for full adaptation
3. **Minimize screen use** â€” Phone lights ruin night vision quickly
4. **Use averted vision** â€” Look slightly away from faint objects
5. **Know the Moon phase** â€” Full moon washes out faint stars

### Astrophotography Notes

- Best during new moon (darkest skies)
- Far from cities (light pollution)
- Clear weather essential
- Stable tripod for long exposures
