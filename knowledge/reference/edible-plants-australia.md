## Universal Edibility Test

**NEVER skip this test for unknown plants**

1. **Inspect** - Check for milky sap, thorns, bitter smell (usually toxic)
2. **Skin Test** - Rub on inner wrist/elbow, wait 15 minutes for reaction
3. **Lip Test** - Touch to lips, wait 3 minutes for burning/tingling
4. **Tongue Test** - Small piece on tongue, wait 15 minutes
5. **Chew Test** - Chew without swallowing, wait 15 minutes
6. **Swallow Test** - Swallow small amount, wait 5 hours
7. **Full Test** - Eat 1/4 cup portion, wait 5 hours
8. **If OK** - Safe to eat in moderate quantities

**STOP immediately if any reaction occurs**

## Common Edible Plants (Native Australian)

### Bush Tucker Staples

#### **Warrigal Greens** (Tetragonia tetragonioides)
- **Identification:** Ground cover, succulent triangular leaves, yellow flowers
- **Edible Parts:** Leaves (young shoots best)
- **Preparation:** MUST boil 5 minutes (contains oxalates), change water, boil again
- **Taste:** Spinach-like
- **Season:** Year-round
- **Nutrition:** High vitamin C, iron
- **Warning:** Never eat raw - kidney stone risk

#### **Native Yam** (Microseris lanceolata)
- **Identification:** Daisy-like yellow flowers, long slender leaves
- **Edible Parts:** Tuberous root
- **Preparation:** Roast or boil
- **Taste:** Sweet, potato-like
- **Season:** Spring-Summer (harvest autumn)
- **Nutrition:** High starch, good calories

#### **Bush Tomato** (Solanum centrale)
- **Identification:** Small shrub, prickly leaves, star-shaped purple flowers
- **Edible Parts:** Ripe fruit only (yellow-brown when ripe)
- **Preparation:** Can eat raw when ripe, or dried
- **Taste:** Caramel-spicy, rich
- **Season:** Summer-Autumn
- **Warning:** Unripe fruit is TOXIC (green = poison)

#### **Pigface** (Carpobrotus glaucescens)
- **Identification:** Succulent, triangular leaves, large pink/purple flowers
- **Edible Parts:** Leaves, fruit, flowers
- **Preparation:** Raw (fruit), leaves can be juiced or cooked
- **Taste:** Salty, slightly tangy
- **Season:** Fruit in summer
- **Nutrition:** Salty (electrolyte replacement)
- **Location:** Coastal areas

#### **Native Mint** (Mentha australis)
- **Identification:** Small leaves, mint smell when crushed, purple/white flowers
- **Edible Parts:** Leaves
- **Preparation:** Raw in salads, tea
- **Taste:** Strong mint flavor
- **Season:** Spring-Summer
- **Use:** Flavoring, tea, medicinal (digestive aid)

## Introduced Edibles (Common in Australia)

### Weeds That Feed

#### **Dandelion** (Taraxacum officinale)
- **Identification:** Jagged leaves, yellow flower, white seed head
- **Edible Parts:** All parts (leaves, flowers, roots)
- **Preparation:** Young leaves raw (older = bitter), roots roasted
- **Taste:** Bitter greens
- **Season:** Year-round (best in spring)
- **Nutrition:** Vitamins A, C, K, calcium

#### **Purslane** (Portulaca oleracea)
- **Identification:** Succulent reddish stems, small oval leaves, yellow flowers
- **Edible Parts:** Stems, leaves
- **Preparation:** Raw or cooked
- **Taste:** Slightly sour, crunchy
- **Season:** Summer-Autumn
- **Nutrition:** Highest omega-3 of any plant, vitamins

#### **Chickweed** (Stellaria media)
- **Identification:** Delicate stems, small oval leaves, tiny white star flowers
- **Edible Parts:** Entire above-ground plant
- **Preparation:** Raw in salads or cooked like spinach
- **Taste:** Mild, slightly sweet
- **Season:** Spring-Autumn
- **Nutrition:** Vitamins A, C, iron

#### **Wood Sorrel** (Oxalis)
- **Identification:** Clover-like leaves (3 heart-shaped leaflets), yellow/white flowers
- **Edible Parts:** Leaves, flowers
- **Preparation:** Raw (small quantities)
- **Taste:** Tart, lemony
- **Season:** Spring-Autumn
- **Warning:** Moderate consumption (oxalic acid)

#### **Miner's Lettuce** (Claytonia perfoliata)
- **Identification:** Round leaf with stem through center, small white flowers
- **Edible Parts:** All above-ground parts
- **Preparation:** Raw in salads
- **Taste:** Mild, lettuce-like
- **Season:** Winter-Spring
- **Nutrition:** High vitamin C

## Native Nuts & Seeds

#### **Macadamia** (Macadamia integrifolia)
- **Identification:** Tree, leathery leaves in whorls, white-pink flowers, hard brown nut
- **Edible Parts:** Nut kernel
- **Preparation:** Crack hard shell (very difficult), eat raw or roasted
- **Season:** Autumn-Winter
- **Nutrition:** High fat, protein, calories

#### **Bunya Pine** (Araucaria bidwillii)
- **Identification:** Large pine tree, huge cones (basketball size)
- **Edible Parts:** Seeds inside cone
- **Preparation:** Roast or boil (like chestnuts)
- **Season:** Autumn (every 3 years major crop)
- **Nutrition:** High starch, protein
- **Warning:** Falling cones can kill - never camp under trees

#### **Wattleseed** (Acacia species - various)
- **Identification:** Many species, look for seed pods on wattle trees
- **Edible Parts:** Seeds (from mature pods)
- **Preparation:** MUST roast to destroy toxins, grind to flour
- **Taste:** Nutty, coffee-like
- **Season:** Summer-Autumn
- **Warning:** Some species toxic - positive ID essential

## Coastal/Water Plants

#### **Sea Lettuce** (Ulva lactuca)
- **Identification:** Bright green, lettuce-like seaweed, thin sheets
- **Edible Parts:** Entire plant
- **Preparation:** Wash well, eat raw or cooked
- **Taste:** Salty, mild
- **Season:** Year-round
- **Nutrition:** Iodine, minerals, vitamins

#### **Samphire** (Salicornia quinqueflora)
- **Identification:** Succulent, segmented stems, no leaves, coastal/salt marsh
- **Edible Parts:** Young stems
- **Preparation:** Raw, pickled, or lightly cooked
- **Taste:** Very salty, crunchy
- **Season:** Spring-Summer
- **Use:** Salt substitute, vegetable

#### **Mangrove** (Avicennia marina - Grey Mangrove)
- **Edible Parts:** Seeds/propagules
- **Preparation:** Soak in water 24-48 hours, change water, boil
- **Taste:** Bland, starchy
- **Season:** Year-round
- **Warning:** Must leach tannins - bitter and astringent otherwise

## TOXIC Plants to AVOID

### Common Deadly Plants in Australia

#### **‚ùå Deadly Nightshade Family**
- Many Solanum species (wild nightshades)
- Green tomato-like fruit = POISON
- Only eat if 100% certain of species

#### **‚ùå Lantana** (Lantana camara)
- Colorful flower clusters (orange, pink, yellow)
- All parts TOXIC, especially berries
- Causes photosensitivity, liver damage

#### **‚ùå Oleander** (Nerium oleander)
- Pink/white flowers, leathery leaves
- EXTREMELY TOXIC - cardiac glycosides
- All parts deadly, don't burn (smoke toxic)

#### **‚ùå Castor Bean** (Ricinus communis)
- Large palmate leaves, spiky seed pods
- Seeds contain ricin (deadly poison)
- 1-2 seeds can be fatal

#### **‚ùå Angel's Trumpet** (Brugmansia)
- Large hanging trumpet flowers
- All parts extremely toxic
- Causes hallucinations, death

#### **‚ùå Bracken Fern** (Pteridium)
- Common fern in bush
- Carcinogenic, causes thiamine deficiency
- Never eat (even cooked)

## Identification Safety Rules

### üî¥ AVOID if plant has:
- Milky or colored sap
- Bitter or almond smell
- Three-leaved growth pattern (often toxic)
- Umbrella-shaped flower clusters (many toxic)
- Spines, fine hairs, or thorns
- Beans, bulbs, or seeds inside pods (unless positively identified)
- Shiny leaves or yellow/white berries

### ‚úÖ SAFER (but still test):
- Aggregate berries (raspberry-like structure)
- Single fruit on stem
- Familiar aromatic smell (mint, citrus)
- Growing in water/wet areas

### üö® EMERGENCY RULE:
**If you can't identify it with 100% certainty, DO NOT EAT IT**

Starvation takes weeks - poisoning takes hours.

## Preparation Methods

### Leaching (Removing Toxins/Bitterness)
1. **Cold Water Leaching:** Slice thin, soak 24-48 hours, change water every 6 hours
2. **Hot Water Leaching:** Boil 10 minutes, discard water, repeat 2-3 times
3. **Wood Ash Leaching:** Soak in wood ash solution, rinse thoroughly

**Use for:** Acorns, some nuts, bitter greens, tannin-rich plants

### Cooking Methods
- **Boiling:** Safest - destroys most toxins and pathogens
- **Roasting:** Good for nuts, seeds, roots
- **Steaming:** Preserves nutrients, good for greens
- **Raw:** Only if 100% certain and tested

## Nutritional Strategy

### Emergency Calorie Sources (Priority)
1. **Nuts** - Highest calories (macadamia, pine nuts)
2. **Seeds** - Good fat and protein (wattleseed)
3. **Roots/Tubers** - Starch/carbs (native yam)
4. **Fruits** - Quick energy (bush tomato, pigface)

### Vitamin Sources
- **Vitamin C:** Warrigal greens, pigface, native mint
- **Vitamin A:** Yellow/orange fruits, dark greens
- **Iron:** Dark greens, some nuts
- **Calcium:** Seaweeds, some greens

### Protein Needs
Plants are LOW in complete protein. Supplement with:
- Insects (grubs, ants)
- Fish/shellfish if coastal
- Small game if possible

- Warrigal greens (hardy)

- Dandelion leaves (less bitter)

## Field Identification Tips

### Look-Alike Warnings
- **Hemlock vs Parsley** - Hemlock has purple spots on stem (DEADLY)
- **Onion/Garlic vs Death Camas** - True onions smell like onion
- **Wild Carrot vs Poison Hemlock** - Hairy stems = carrot, smooth = poison

### Carry These Tools
- Field guide (Australian plants)
- Magnifying glass
- Small knife for testing
- Notebook for recording finds
