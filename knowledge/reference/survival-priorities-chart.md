## Rule of 3s (Survival Timeline)

| Priority | Timeline | Critical Actions |
|----------|----------|------------------|
| **Air** | 3 minutes | Clear airway, CPR, breathing assistance |
| **Shelter** | 3 hours | Protection from elements, hypothermia/hyperthermia prevention |
| **Water** | 3 days | Find, purify, store water sources |
| **Food** | 3 weeks | Foraging, hunting, fishing, rationing |
| **Hope/Morale** | 3 months | Mental health, purpose, community |

## STOP Survival Protocol

**S - STOP**
- Stop moving immediately
- Sit down, calm breathing
- Don't panic or make rash decisions

**T - THINK**
- Assess your situation honestly
- Check for injuries
- Inventory resources and tools
- Evaluate environment and hazards

**O - OBSERVE**
- Survey surroundings 360Â°
- Identify water sources
- Note shelter locations
- Check weather patterns
- Look for rescue signs

**P - PLAN**
- Prioritize immediate needs (Rule of 3s)
- Create action checklist
- Set realistic goals
- Establish timeline
- Prepare contingencies

## Resource Rationing Guide

### Water Rationing (Limited Supply)
| Supply Duration | Daily Ration | Strategy |
|-----------------|--------------|----------|
| 1-2 days | No rationing | Find new source immediately |
| 3-5 days | 50% reduction | Minimize exertion, find source |
| 6-10 days | 75% reduction | Emergency conservation mode |
| >10 days | Survival minimum | Critical - all effort to water |

**Rationing Tips:**
- Drink at regular intervals (don't gulp)
- Avoid alcohol and caffeine
- Minimize sweating (work in cool hours)
- Moisten lips, don't waste drinking
- Use water efficiently (cooking, washing)

### Food Rationing
| Supply Duration | Daily Calories | Strategy |
|-----------------|----------------|----------|
| 1-3 days | Normal intake | Establish foraging/hunting |
| 4-7 days | 75% intake | Begin supplementing |
| 1-2 weeks | 50% intake | Focus on high-fat foods |
| 2-4 weeks | 25-50% | Survival mode, conserve energy |

## Energy Conservation

### Activity Energy Costs (Relative)
| Activity | Energy Cost | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Resting | 1x baseline | Lying down, minimal movement |
| Sitting | 1.2x | Light tasks, planning |
| Walking (flat) | 3-4x | Normal pace |
| Walking (terrain) | 5-8x | Hills, obstacles |
| Running | 8-12x | Avoid unless emergency |
| Shelter building | 6-10x | Heavy work |
| Digging | 10-15x | Extreme exertion |

**Energy Conservation Rules:**
1. Work during coolest hours (dawn/dusk)
2. Rest during heat of day
3. Pace yourself - slow and steady
4. Avoid unnecessary movement
5. Plan routes efficiently
6. Share work in groups

## Dehydration Assessment

| % Dehydration | Symptoms | Functional Impact | Urgency |
|---------------|----------|-------------------|---------|
| 1-2% | Thirst, reduced performance | Mild cognitive decline | Monitor |
| 3-4% | Dry mouth, fatigue | Significant performance loss | Find water |
| 5-6% | Dizziness, headache | Coordination problems | Urgent |
| 7-8% | Severe weakness | Incapacitation risk | Critical |
| 9-10% | Muscle spasms, delirium | Imminent collapse | Emergency |
| >10% | Organ failure | Death risk | Life-threatening |

**Field Test:** Pinch skin on back of hand - if it stays "tented" for >2 seconds, you're dehydrated.

## Medical Emergency Priority (MARCH)

**M - Massive Hemorrhage**
- Direct pressure
- Tourniquet if limb
- Life-threatening bleeding first

**A - Airway**
- Clear obstruction
- Head tilt/chin lift
- Recovery position

**R - Respiration**
- Check breathing
- Rescue breaths if needed
- Chest seal for punctures

**C - Circulation**
- Check pulse
- CPR if needed
- Treat for shock

**H - Hypothermia/Head Injury**
- Prevent heat loss
- Monitor consciousness
- Avoid moving if spinal injury

## Environmental Hazard Quick Reference

| Hazard | Recognition | Immediate Action | Prevention |
|--------|-------------|------------------|------------|
| Flash Flood | Distant thunder, rising water | High ground immediately | Avoid canyons, dry washes in rain |
| Avalanche | Cracking snow, rapid movement | Swim upward, create air pocket | Avoid steep slopes after snow |
| Lightning | 30-second thunder rule | Crouch low, avoid trees | Seek low ground, ditch trees |
| Wildfire | Smoke, ash, heat | Upwind/uphill escape | Create firebreak, wet down |
