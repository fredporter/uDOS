# Basic Budgeting & Financial Literacy

**Category:** Skills - Financial Management
**Priority:** HIGH - Foundation for self-reliance
**Application:** Daily, weekly, monthly planning
**Difficulty:** Beginner to Intermediate

---

## Overview

Financial literacy is a survival skill. Whether facing economic hardship, planning for emergencies, or building security, understanding money management is essential.

**This guide covers:**
- Income and expense tracking
- Budget creation methods
- Emergency fund building
- Debt management
- Australian-specific financial context
- Practical tools and strategies

**Key principle:** It's not about how much you earn, it's about managing what you have.

## Understanding Your Financial Situation

### Income Assessment

**Gross vs Net Income:**

```
╔════════════════════╦═══════════════════════════════╗
║ INCOME TYPE       ║ DESCRIPTION                   ║
╠════════════════════╬═══════════════════════════════╣
║ Gross Income      ║ Before tax, super, deductions ║
║                   ║ (what employer pays)          ║
╠════════════════════╬═══════════════════════════════╣
║ Net Income        ║ After tax, super, deductions  ║
║ ("Take-home pay") ║ (what hits your account)      ║
╚════════════════════╩═══════════════════════════════╝

Budget on NET income only (what you actually receive)
```

---

**Income Sources:**

```
Regular income:
├─ Employment (wages/salary)
├─ Self-employment/business
├─ Government benefits (Centrelink)
├─ Superannuation (pension phase)
├─ Rental income
└─ Investment dividends

Irregular income:
├─ Bonuses
├─ Overtime
├─ Freelance/casual work
├─ Tax refunds
└─ Gifts

For budgeting:
└─ Use LOWEST regular amount (conservative approach)
   Don't budget on overtime/bonuses (bonus = savings/debt reduction)
```

## Expense Tracking

### The 30-Day Challenge

**Before budgeting, TRACK everything for one month:**

```
Method 1: Notebook/spreadsheet
├─ Date | Item | Amount | Category
├─ Small purchases matter! (coffee, snacks)
├─ Cash AND card purchases
└─ Write it down immediately (memory fails)

Method 2: Banking app
├─ Most banks categorize transactions
├─ Review weekly
├─ Adjust categories
└─ Export to spreadsheet

Method 3: Budgeting app
├─ Pocketbook (free, connects to bank)
├─ YNAB (You Need A Budget) - paid
├─ Frollo (free)
└─ MoneyBrilliant (free)

What you'll discover:
├─ Where money actually goes (surprises!)
├─ Subscriptions forgotten
├─ Small leaks (daily coffee = $1,500/year)
└─ Spending patterns (emotional, habitual)
```

## Budget Creation Methods

### 50/30/20 Rule (Simple)

**Allocate net income:**

```
50% NEEDS (essentials):
├─ Rent/mortgage
├─ Utilities
├─ Groceries
├─ Transport
├─ Insurance
├─ Minimum loan payments
└─ Healthcare

30% WANTS (discretionary):
├─ Eating out
├─ Entertainment
├─ Hobbies
├─ Subscriptions (beyond essential)
├─ Shopping
└─ Holidays

20% SAVINGS & DEBT:
├─ Emergency fund
├─ Super contributions (extra)
├─ Savings goals
├─ Extra debt payments
└─ Investments

Example: $4,000/month net income
├─ Needs: $2,000
├─ Wants: $1,200
└─ Savings: $800
```

**Adjust ratios if needed:**
- High rent area: 60/20/20 or 70/15/15
- Debt focus: 50/20/30 (more to debt reduction)
- Young/no dependents: 50/30/20 works well

### Envelope System (Cash-Based)

**Physical envelopes for variable expenses:**

```
How it works:
1. Determine monthly budget for each category
2. Withdraw cash at start of month
3. Put cash in labeled envelopes
4. Spend ONLY from appropriate envelope
5. When empty, stop spending in that category

Example envelopes:
├─ Groceries: $600
├─ Fuel: $200
├─ Eating out: $150
├─ Entertainment: $100
├─ Personal: $100
└─ Misc: $50

Advantages:
├─ Physical limit (can see remaining)
├─ No overspending (can't spend what's not there)
├─ Awareness (handing over cash more conscious)
└─ Simple tracking

Disadvantages:
├─ Need to carry cash
├─ No rewards points
├─ Inconvenient for some purchases
└─ Security (loss/theft)

Modern hybrid:
└─ Use debit cards but check "envelope" balance (spreadsheet/app)
```

---

### Reverse Budgeting (Pay Yourself First)

**Savings come first:**

```
Income arrives →
  ↓
Immediately transfer to savings (automatic) →
  ↓
Live on what's left

Example: $4,500 income
├─ Auto-transfer $900 to savings (20%)
├─ Live on $3,600
└─ Adjust spending to fit

Advantages:
├─ Savings happen (not leftover)
├─ Forces frugality
├─ Builds wealth automatically
└─ Simple

Setup:
├─ Separate savings account (high interest)
├─ Automatic transfer on payday
├─ Make it slightly inconvenient to access (prevents raiding)
└─ Review quarterly
```

## Emergency Fund

**Your financial safety net:**

### Why It's Critical

```
Emergency fund prevents:
├─ Debt spiral (using credit card for emergency)
├─ Panic decisions
├─ Selling investments at loss
├─ Borrowing from family
└─ Financial stress

Common emergencies:
├─ Job loss
├─ Medical expenses
├─ Car breakdown
├─ Appliance failure
├─ Urgent travel
└─ Unexpected bills
```

### How Much?

**Target levels:**

```
╔════════════════════╦═══════════════════════════════╗
║ STAGE             ║ TARGET                        ║
╠════════════════════╬═══════════════════════════════╣
║ Starter           ║ $1,000-$2,000                 ║
║ (first goal)      ║ Covers most small emergencies ║
╠════════════════════╬═══════════════════════════════╣
║ Basic             ║ 3 months expenses             ║
║                   ║ Job loss buffer               ║
╠════════════════════╬═══════════════════════════════╣
║ Comfortable       ║ 6 months expenses             ║
║                   ║ Extended job search, recovery ║
╠════════════════════╬═══════════════════════════════╣
║ Very secure       ║ 12 months expenses            ║
║                   ║ Major crisis, career change   ║
╚════════════════════╩═══════════════════════════════╝

Calculate YOUR target:
Monthly essential expenses × 3 (or 6, or 12)

Example:
├─ Rent: $1,400
├─ Utilities: $250
├─ Food: $600
├─ Transport: $300
├─ Insurance: $100
├─ Phone: $40
└─ Total: $2,690/month

3-month fund: $2,690 × 3 = $8,070
6-month fund: $2,690 × 6 = $16,140
```

### Building Your Fund

**Even small amounts add up:**

```
Strategy:
├─ Start with $1,000 goal
├─ Find $50-100/week to redirect
├─ Automatic transfer (payday)
├─ Add windfalls (tax refund, bonus)
└─ Don't touch except emergency

Progress example ($100/week):
├─ Week 10: $1,000 ✓ (starter fund complete)
├─ Week 20: $2,000
├─ Month 6: $2,600
├─ Month 12: $5,200
├─ Month 18: $7,800
└─ Month 21: $8,400 ✓ (3-month fund complete)

Where to find $100/week:
├─ Cancel subscriptions ($20)
├─ Reduce eating out ($40)
├─ Meal prep (save $30)
├─ Reduce energy use ($10)
└─ Total: $100
```

## Debt Management

### Good Debt vs Bad Debt

**Good debt** (builds assets):

```
├─ Home loan (appreciating asset, tax benefits)
├─ HECS-HELP (low interest, income-contingent)
├─ Investment loan (rental property, shares - with caution)
└─ Business loan (generates income)

Characteristics:
├─ Low interest rate
├─ Tax deductible (sometimes)
├─ Builds wealth
└─ Manageable payments
```

---

**Bad debt** (depreciating or consumption):

```
├─ Credit card (high interest, often 20%+)
├─ Personal loans (consumption)
├─ Pay-day loans (AVOID! 400%+ equivalent)
├─ Afterpay/ZipPay (easy overspending)
└─ Car loans (depreciating asset)

Characteristics:
├─ High interest
├─ No tax benefit
├─ Doesn't build wealth
└─ Stress
```

---

### Debt Payoff Strategies

**Snowball Method** (psychological wins):

```
1. List all debts (smallest to largest balance)
2. Minimum payment on all debts
3. Extra payments to SMALLEST debt
4. When smallest paid off, roll payment to next smallest
5. Repeat

Example:
├─ Credit card 1: $800 (minimum $25)
├─ Credit card 2: $3,000 (minimum $75)
├─ Personal loan: $8,000 (minimum $200)
└─ Total available: $400/month

Month 1-4:
├─ CC1: $225 ($25 min + $200 extra) → PAID OFF month 4
├─ CC2: $75 (minimum)
├─ Loan: $200 (minimum)

Month 5 onwards:
├─ CC2: $300 ($75 min + $225 rolled from CC1) → PAID OFF month 15
├─ Loan: $200 (minimum)

Month 16 onwards:
├─ Loan: $500 (all money focused here) → PAID OFF month 32

Total: 32 months debt-free

Advantage: Quick wins (motivation)
Disadvantage: Not mathematically optimal (higher interest debts might be larger)
```

---

**Avalanche Method** (mathematically optimal):

```
1. List all debts (highest to lowest interest rate)
2. Minimum payment on all debts
3. Extra payments to HIGHEST interest debt
4. When paid off, roll to next highest interest
5. Repeat

Same example, but sorted by interest:
├─ Credit card 1: $800 @ 22% (minimum $25)
├─ Credit card 2: $3,000 @ 20% (minimum $75)
├─ Personal loan: $8,000 @ 12% (minimum $200)
└─ Total available: $400/month

Focus on CC1 first (highest interest), then CC2, then loan.

Advantage: Saves most money (less interest paid)
Disadvantage: Slower psychological wins (if high-interest debt is large)
```

---

**Hybrid Approach:**

```
1. Pay off one small debt (quick win, motivation)
2. Switch to avalanche (highest interest)
3. Best of both worlds
```

---

### Credit Card Debt Trap

**The compounding problem:**

```
$3,000 credit card @ 20% p.a.

Paying minimum only ($75/month):
├─ Time to pay off: 6.5 YEARS
├─ Total interest: $2,850
└─ Total paid: $5,850 (almost double!)

Paying $200/month:
├─ Time to pay off: 18 months
├─ Total interest: $540
└─ Total paid: $3,540

Extra $125/month saves:
├─ $2,310 in interest
└─ 5 years of payments
```

**Credit card rules:**

```
DO:
├─ Pay full balance each month (no interest)
├─ Use for rewards/protection (if disciplined)
├─ Track spending carefully
└─ Set up automatic payment (at least minimum)

DON'T:
├─ Carry balance (20%+ interest is wealth destruction)
├─ Use for things you can't afford
├─ Only pay minimum
├─ Cash advances (even higher interest + fees)
└─ Multiple cards (unless managed carefully)
```

## Saving Strategies

### Automatic Transfers

**Set and forget:**

```
Payday → Automatic transfers:
├─ Emergency fund: $X
├─ Bills account: $Y
├─ Savings goals: $Z
└─ What's left = spending money

Accounts setup:
├─ Everyday (spending): Debit card access
├─ Bills (fixed expenses): Auto-payments from here
├─ Emergency fund: High interest, no card
├─ Savings goals: Separate sub-accounts
└─ All at same bank (free transfers) or use services like Up, ING
```

---

### The "Round-Up" Method

**Micro-savings:**

```
Some banks/apps round purchases to nearest dollar, save difference:

$4.30 coffee → $5.00 charged → $0.70 to savings
$23.80 groceries → $24.00 charged → $0.20 to savings
$156.20 bill → $157.00 charged → $0.80 to savings

Average 10-20 transactions/week × $0.50 = $5-10/week = $260-520/year

Not huge, but:
├─ Painless (don't notice)
├─ Automatic
└─ Adds up
```

---

### Savings Goals

**Make it concrete:**

```
Instead of: "Save money"
Set: "Save $5,000 for emergency fund by Dec 2026"

Goal template:
├─ What: (emergency fund, holiday, car, house deposit)
├─ How much: ($5,000)
├─ By when: (12 months)
├─ Per month: ($5,000 ÷ 12 = $417)
└─ Per week: ($417 ÷ 4.33 = $96)

Track progress:
├─ Visual tracker (chart, thermometer)
├─ Celebrate milestones (25%, 50%, 75%)
└─ Adjust if needed
```

## Reducing Expenses

### The 30-Day Rule

**For non-essential purchases:**

```
Want something? Wait 30 days.
├─ Add to list with date
├─ If still want after 30 days, buy it
├─ Often realize you don't need it
└─ Eliminates impulse purchases

Result: Save 50-80% on discretionary spending
```

---

### Subscription Audit

**The forgotten money drain:**

```
Common subscriptions:
├─ Streaming (Netflix, Stan, Disney+, etc.)
├─ Music (Spotify, Apple)
├─ Gym memberships (unused?)
├─ Apps/games
├─ News/magazines
├─ Cloud storage
└─ Meal kits

Action:
1. List ALL subscriptions (check bank statements)
2. Calculate annual cost
3. Ask: "Do I use this weekly? Is it worth it?"
4. Cancel unused/low-value
5. Consider sharing (family plans)

Example savings:
├─ Netflix (not watching): $17/month = $204/year
├─ Gym (going 1×/month): $60/month = $720/year
├─ App subscriptions (forgotten): $15/month = $180/year
└─ Total: $1,104/year saved
```

### Energy/Utilities

**Small changes, big impact:**

```
Electricity:
├─ LED bulbs (use 75% less)
├─ Turn off standby devices
├─ Cold water laundry
├─ Hang dry (not dryer)
├─ Efficient appliances (when replacing)
├─ Compare plans (Energy Made Easy - govt site)
└─ Potential: $200-400/year

Water:
├─ Short showers (5 min vs 10 min)
├─ Fix leaks (dripping tap = $100/year)
├─ Full loads (washing machine, dishwasher)
└─ Potential: $100-200/year

Internet/Phone:
├─ Call and negotiate (retention offers)
├─ Switch providers (new customer deals)
├─ Downgrade plan (do you need 100Mbps?)
└─ Potential: $100-300/year
```

### Food Expenses

**Biggest variable expense:**

```
Reduce costs without suffering:
├─ Meal plan (weekly)
├─ Shopping list (stick to it)
├─ Shop after eating (not hungry)
├─ Cook in batches (freeze portions)
├─ Reduce meat (expensive protein)
├─ Buy in-season produce
├─ Store brands vs name brands
├─ Reduce takeaway (biggest leak)
└─ Pack lunch for work

Example:
├─ Buy lunch daily: $15 × 5 = $75/week = $3,900/year
├─ Pack lunch: $5 × 5 = $25/week = $1,300/year
└─ Savings: $2,600/year

Grocery efficiency:
├─ Aldi/Costco for staples (cheaper)
├─ Fruit/veg markets (fresher, cheaper)
├─ Bulk buy pantry staples
└─ Check unit prices (per 100g, not package)
```

**Retirement savings (compulsory):**

```
How it works:
├─ Employer contributes 11.5% of salary (not from your pay)
├─ You can add extra (voluntary contributions)
├─ Tax advantages (15% tax in super vs up to 47% outside)

├─ myGov (link to ATO)
├─ Are you in right fund? (compare fees, returns)

├─ Check beneficiaries

└─ First Home Super Saver Scheme (contribute to super, withdraw for house)
```

## Budget Troubleshooting

**Common problems:**

```
"I can't stick to budget"
├─ Too restrictive? (Allow some fun money)
├─ Not tracking? (Can't manage what you don't measure)
├─ Unrealistic categories? (Review actual spending)
└─ Need accountability? (Budget buddy, app notifications)

"Unexpected expenses keep derailing me"
├─ Build buffer category (5-10% of budget)
├─ Review "unexpected" (car service is predictable, pro-rate)
├─ Build emergency fund (prevents crisis)
└─ Track for 3 months (patterns emerge)

"Income varies (casual/self-employed)"
├─ Budget on lowest typical month
├─ Extra income months → savings/debt
├─ Build larger emergency fund (6-12 months)
└─ Smooth income (pay yourself salary from business account)

"Partner won't budget"
├─ Start with yourself (model behavior)
├─ Share goals (what do WE want?)
├─ Find compromise (some discretionary for each)
├─ Regular money dates (monthly check-in, not accusatory)
└─ Celebrate wins together
```

## Budget Review Process

**Monthly review (30 minutes):**

```
1. Actual vs budgeted
   ├─ What categories over/under?
   ├─ Why? (one-off or pattern?)
   └─ Adjust next month

2. Progress on goals
   ├─ Emergency fund growing?
   ├─ Debt reducing?
   └─ Celebrate progress!

3. Upcoming expenses
   ├─ Next month different? (birthday, holiday)
   ├─ Adjust categories
   └─ Plan for irregular expenses

4. Wins and challenges
   ├─ What worked well?
   ├─ What was difficult?
   └─ How to improve?

Quarterly review:
├─ Big picture progress
├─ Adjust long-term goals
├─ Check emergency fund adequacy
└─ Review all subscriptions/contracts
```

**Key Principles:**

1. **Track first, budget second.** Can't manage what you don't measure.
2. **Pay yourself first.** Savings are priority, not leftover.
3. **Emergency fund is foundation.** Build this before aggressive debt payoff.
4. **Small consistent changes >> big bursts.** $20/week saved = $1,040/year.
5. **Budget is plan, not prison.** Adjust as needed, life changes.
